# Skill: Show, Don't Tell

Rewrite a vague, general draft into something specific and believable.

## Rules
- Replace generic claims with concrete details: real numbers, names of things, specific moments, exact quotes.
- Turn "we improved results" into the actual before and after.
- Anchor abstract points to a single scene or example the reader can picture.
- Keep it brief but make it visceral. Cut adjectives, add evidence.
- Do not invent facts. Where a specific is missing, mark it as [add specific: ...] so the writer can fill it in.

## Output
Return the rewritten draft, with any [add specific: ...] placeholders where real detail is needed.
