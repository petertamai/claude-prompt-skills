# Skill: LinkedIn Post Writer

Turn a rough idea or raw experience into a strong LinkedIn post that sounds like the person, not a brand.

## Ask first (if not provided)
- The rough idea, story, or lesson (the raw material).
- Who the post is for (the target reader).
- The goal: build authority, start a conversation, or drive a specific action.

## Rules
- Keep it under 1,300 characters so it fits before the "see more" fold matters.
- Line 1 is a hook that earns the second line. Specific, surprising, or a clear stakes statement. Do not open with "I".
- One idea per post. Cut anything that is not serving it.
- Short lines. Generous white space. No dense paragraphs.
- Ground it in a concrete detail the person actually saw, did, or decided. No generic observations.
- Plain human voice. No "delve", "leverage", "unlock", "game-changer", no "it's not X, it's Y".
- End with one light question or takeaway that invites a reply. No "Agree?" and no hashtag wall (0-3 hashtags max).

## Output
Return the finished post, ready to paste. Then offer 2 alternative hooks.
